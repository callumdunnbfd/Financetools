Affiliate Disclosure

How affiliate links work on this site, and how they help fund the tools.

Last updated: 1 March 2026

Plain English: Some links are affiliate links. If you click and take a qualifying action, we may earn a commission. This does not usually increase your cost.

How affiliate links work

When you click an affiliate link, the destination provider (or their affiliate network) may use tracking (for example, cookies or similar technologies) to attribute your visit to us. If you later complete a qualifying action (such as an application, eligibility check, sign-up, or purchase), we may receive a commission.

Does this cost you more?

Typically, no. Affiliate commissions are generally paid by the provider. Your price is usually the same whether you use our link or not.

How we choose what to show

  • We aim to show options relevant to the calculator result (for example, reducing interest or improving savings rate).
  • Compensation can influence which partners we feature and how links are presented.
  • We do not guarantee eligibility, acceptance, pricing, or outcomes.

Not financial advice

We provide tools and general information only. We do not provide personalised financial advice. Consider your circumstances and, if needed, seek advice from a qualified professional.

Partner responsibility

Partner websites are responsible for their products, offers, eligibility checks, application processes, and compliance obligations. Always review their terms, fees, and conditions before applying or purchasing.
